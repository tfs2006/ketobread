Bake Like a Pro

5 Famous Keto Bread Recipes

Discover the most popular low-carb bread recipes that have helped thousands enjoy bread on their keto journey. From quick microwave breads to artisan-style loaves.

Bread Without the Carbs

These recipes have become famous in the keto community for delivering real bread taste and texture without the carbs. Each recipe uses different techniques and ingredients to achieve that satisfying bread experience.

1

90-Second Keto Bread

The quickest keto bread you'll ever make

Prep Time: 2 minutes
Cook Time: 90 seconds
Net Carbs: ~2g per serving

The 90-second bread is a keto community favorite for good reason—it's incredibly fast and satisfies that bread craving in under two minutes. Made in a mug in the microwave, it's perfect for toast, sandwiches, or a quick bread fix.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons almond flour
  • 1 tablespoon butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Mix all ingredients in a microwave-safe mug until smooth.
  2. Microwave on high for 90 seconds.
  3. Let cool for 1 minute, then slide out of the mug.
  4. Slice in half for sandwich bread or toast as desired.

Pro Tips

For better texture, let the bread cool completely before slicing. Toast it in a pan with butter for a crispy exterior. Add herbs or cheese to the batter for variety.

2

Fathead Dough Bread

The versatile dough that changed keto baking

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 12-15 minutes
Net Carbs: ~3g per serving

Fathead dough originated from the documentary "Fat Head" and has become the most famous keto dough recipe. Its cheese-based formula creates a pliable, pizza-like dough that works for bread rolls, pizza crusts, bagels, and more.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tablespoons cream cheese
  • ¾ cup almond flour
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon Italian herbs (optional)

Instructions

  1. Melt mozzarella and cream cheese together in the microwave (about 1 minute, stirring halfway).
  2. Add almond flour and egg to the melted cheese. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough until smooth (wet your hands to prevent sticking).
  4. Shape into rolls or a loaf and place on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  5. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 12-15 minutes until golden brown.

Pro Tips

Work quickly while the dough is warm—it becomes harder to shape as it cools. If it gets too stiff, microwave for 10-15 seconds. This dough is excellent for making keto bagels, breadsticks, and calzones.

3

Cloud Bread (Oopsie Bread)

Light, fluffy, and almost zero carbs

Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 25-30 minutes
Net Carbs: ~0.5g per piece

Cloud bread, also known as "oopsie bread," is one of the oldest and most popular keto bread alternatives. Made primarily from eggs and cream cheese, it's incredibly light and airy—perfect for burger buns or sandwich bread with virtually no carbs.

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 3 tablespoons cream cheese, softened
  • ¼ teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 tablespoon sweetener (optional, for sweet version)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Beat egg whites with cream of tartar until stiff peaks form.
  3. In another bowl, mix egg yolks with cream cheese until smooth.
  4. Gently fold the egg white mixture into the yolk mixture.
  5. Spoon onto baking sheet in 6 rounds (about ¾ inch thick).
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.

Pro Tips

Don't overmix when folding—you want to keep the air in the egg whites. These are best eaten the same day but can be stored in the fridge for 3-4 days. Toast them lightly to restore crispness.

4

Almond Flour Sandwich Bread

The closest to "real" bread texture

Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 30-35 minutes
Net Carbs: ~2g per slice

This almond flour bread is designed to mimic traditional sandwich bread as closely as possible. It slices well, toasts beautifully, and has a soft, bread-like crumb that's perfect for sandwiches, toast, or French toast.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups almond flour (finely ground)
  • 4 large eggs
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  2. Whisk together almond flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat eggs, then add melted butter and vinegar.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients until smooth.
  5. Pour into prepared pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Cool in pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

Pro Tips

Use finely ground almond flour (not almond meal) for the best texture. Let the bread cool completely before slicing to prevent crumbling. This bread freezes well—slice it first for easy grab-and-go portions.

5

Coconut Flour Bread

Nut-free and budget-friendly

Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 45-50 minutes
Net Carbs: ~2g per slice

Coconut flour bread is the perfect option for those with nut allergies or anyone looking for a more affordable keto bread. Coconut flour is highly absorbent, so this recipe uses more eggs to create a moist, tender loaf with a subtle sweetness.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup coconut flour
  • 6 large eggs
  • ½ cup butter, melted (or coconut oil)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sweetener (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 9x5 inch loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. Whisk eggs in a large bowl until well beaten.
  3. Add melted butter and mix well.
  4. Sift in coconut flour, baking powder, and salt. Mix until no lumps remain.
  5. Let batter rest for 5 minutes (coconut flour absorbs liquid).
  6. Pour into prepared pan.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes until golden and firm to touch.
  8. Cool completely before slicing.

Pro Tips

Always sift coconut flour to avoid lumps. Don't skip the resting time—it helps the coconut flour absorb the liquid properly. This bread can taste slightly eggy; adding a touch of vanilla extract helps mask the egg flavor.
